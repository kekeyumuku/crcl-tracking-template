# CRCL Tracking Template

> Purpose: not to predict the stock, but to continuously judge whether CRCL is still mainly a spread-driven issuer or is evolving into a payments infrastructure platform.

> Core rule: **If it doesn’t change the model, don’t promote it to the card.**

## Who this is for

- Investors tracking Circle / CRCL
- Researchers studying stablecoins and payments infrastructure
- Anyone trying to separate profit volatility from business-model migration

## What problem this solves

Many tracking notes mix together:

- quarterly profit changes
- rate-cycle effects
- market sentiment
- regulatory narrative
- platformization progress

This template separates those layers and only tracks information that changes one of these three things:

1. **the base**
2. **the upside**
3. **the company’s business identity**

## How to use

Whenever you see new information related to CRCL / USDC / stablecoin regulation / enterprise payments adoption, start by filling the quick version first.

Only promote it to a full card if it actually changes the model.

## The 8 core dimensions

The first 4 mainly evaluate the **current base**, and the last 4 mainly evaluate the **long-term upside**.

### A. Base
1. **USDC circulation**
2. **Growth quality**
3. **Rate sensitivity**
4. **Revenue retention / channel economics**

### B. Upside
5. **Non-spread revenue**
6. **Market share**
7. **Regulatory position**
8. **Enterprise / payments adoption**

## Calibration notes

### Growth quality
- **Low**: driven mainly by short-term trading activity, one-off flows, or bursty demand
- **Medium**: expanding into multiple use cases, but persistence is still unproven
- **High**: increasingly driven by more durable demand such as enterprise, payments, or cross-border usage

### Platform-revenue signals
Usually means early validation of one or more of these:
- API / service fees
- enterprise account capabilities
- payment orchestration or settlement capabilities
- durable fee take enabled by network position

### Real workflow adoption
A partnership announcement alone does not count.
Look for evidence that USDC or Circle capabilities are entering real workflows such as:
- collections / payouts / settlement
- reconciliation / treasury / liquidity movement
- repeatable transaction flow or recurring revenue logic

## First question: does this deserve a formal card update?

Only formalize information if it changes one of these:

- **the base**: circulation, growth source, rate sensitivity, or channel economics change in a meaningful way
- **upside validation**: stronger signs of platform revenue, real enterprise/payments adoption, regulatory advancement, or market-share gains in key use cases
- **competitive position**: Circle’s edge is validated, weakened, or copied

Information that usually does **not** deserve a formal card update:

- pure narrative
- pure roadmap
- pure sentiment
- vision statements with no supporting data or real-world adoption

**Rule: if it doesn’t change the model, don’t log it formally.**

- **Upside**: non-spread revenue, market share, regulation, enterprise adoption
- **Real inflection point**: whether the business is moving from spread capture to platform rent capture

---

## Common mistakes
- treating short-term USDC growth as long-term platform validation
- treating friendly regulation language as real adoption
- treating partnership announcements as real workflow integration
- treating better rate conditions as company-specific moat strengthening
- treating valuation narrative shifts as proof that business structure already changed
